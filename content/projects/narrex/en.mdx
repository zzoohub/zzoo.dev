A Korean web novelist knows their story. The protagonist who returns to the past, the system window that appears, the twist in episode 3 that changes everything. The world is already built — fully formed — inside their head.

What's missing is a production system to get it out. Forty episodes means hundreds of scenes to organize, characters who must remember what happened thirty chapters ago, and 3,000 to 5,000 words of prose per episode, every week. The bottleneck was never creativity — it was finding a tool that works at the unit writers actually think in: **scenes, characters, and plot arcs.**

## The timeline is the prompt

Narrex is a visual editor where you arrange scenes on a multi-track timeline, build a character relationship map, and set genre, tone, and point of view. AI drafts prose scene by scene — using the timeline itself as the prompt. No prompt engineering. No copy-pasting context into a chat window.

Every scene knows who's in it, what happened before, and what's happening simultaneously on other tracks. Episode 40 stays consistent with episode 1 — without a separate story bible.

## From idea to structured project in seconds

1. **Paste a story idea** — a paragraph, a Notion dump, a one-liner
2. **Get a full structure** — settings, a scene-populated timeline, a character map with relationships
3. **Arrange visually** — drag scenes, add parallel tracks, edit characters
4. **Generate per scene** — AI drafts with full narrative context
5. **Direct and refine** — edit freely, or give directions: "more tension," "increase dialogue"

## Built for Korean web novel conventions

Not a translated English tool. Designed from the ground up for regression-fantasy, romance, murim, and other Korean web novel genres. A sample project on signup — 2 tracks, 8 scenes, 4 characters, regression-fantasy — lets you explore the system before committing your own story.
