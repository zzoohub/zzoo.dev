## Have a novel in your head that never makes it to the page?

You know the story. The characters have names. You can picture the opening scene. But between the idea and a finished manuscript, there are 40 episodes of structuring, consistency-checking, and writing 3,000-5,000 characters of prose per episode.

Most aspiring novelists don't lack creativity. They lack a production system.

## The wrong tools at the wrong level

Existing AI writing tools operate at the wrong granularity. **Sentence-level autocomplete** (like Sudowrite) makes you do all the structural work and just finishes your sentences. **Fully automated generators** (like ChatGPT for writing) remove your creative control entirely and lose consistency after a few exchanges.

Neither respects how web novelists actually think: in **scenes, characters, and plot arcs** — not in individual sentences or entire chapters.

## Your timeline is the prompt

Narrex gives you a **visual timeline editor** where you arrange your story's structure — scenes, characters, relationships, and plot points — then generate prose scene by scene. The timeline itself becomes the AI prompt. No prompt engineering required.

Set the genre, tone, era, and POV once in the Config Bar, and every generation inherits that context automatically. Add a character to the Character Map, and every scene they appear in knows who they are.

## How it works

1. **Paste your story idea** — any free-form concept, even a rough paragraph
2. **Get auto-generated structure** — config, timeline, and character map created instantly
3. **Arrange scenes on the timeline** — drag, reorder, add parallel tracks for simultaneous storylines
4. **Generate prose per scene** — AI drafts each scene with full context: config, characters, prior summaries, and simultaneous events
5. **Direct and refine** — edit freely or give direction-based revision like "add more tension" or "increase dialogue"

## Built for Korean web novels

Narrex isn't a translated English tool. It's built from the ground up for **Korean web novel genres, tropes, and conventions** — regression-fantasy, romance, murim, and more. The sample project on signup is a regression-fantasy with 2 tracks, 8 scenes, and 4-5 characters, so you can see how the system works before creating your own.

Full context awareness means your 40th episode stays consistent with your 1st — without you managing a separate story bible.
