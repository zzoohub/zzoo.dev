## Core Insight

Writers think in scenes and plot arcs — not sentences. Every existing AI writing tool ignores this. Sentence-level autocomplete makes you do all the structural work. Fully automated generators strip your creative control. Neither addresses the real bottleneck: developing dozens of interconnected scenes into a consistent, multi-episode narrative.

Narrex makes the scene the unit of AI generation, and the visual structure the prompt.

## Who This Is For

**Ji-yeon — The Aspiring Writer**

University student with a regression-fantasy story she's been imagining for months. Characters have names, the opening is vivid in her head. But between the idea and 40 episodes of finished prose, there's a wall she's never climbed. She tried ChatGPT — output was inconsistent after a few chapters. She doesn't lack creativity. She lacks a production system.

*"I want to turn my story idea into a real, readable novel — without needing to master prose writing."*

**Min-ho — The Struggling Serializer**

Published 8 episodes on Novelpia. Readers liked the premise but caught inconsistencies — a character's eye color changed, a subplot was forgotten. He writes 2–3 hours per night and barely produces one episode per week. He knows his story. He can't maintain quality at the pace readers expect.

*"I want the tool to remember everything that happened before and generate a draft that's consistent with my established story."*

## The Core Loop

Every design decision flows from one user journey:

1. **Paste a story idea** — any starting point, even a rough paragraph
2. **Get auto-generated structure** — config, timeline with scenes, character map with relationships
3. **Arrange the story visually** — drag scenes, add tracks for parallel storylines, edit characters
4. **Generate prose per scene** — AI assembles full context automatically and drafts the scene
5. **Direct and refine** — edit freely, or give direction like "add more tension"

Three clicks to first value: create project, enter idea, click "Structure My Story." The hardest part of novel writing — going from "I have an idea" to "I have a plan" — happens in the first session.

## Key Product Decisions

### Scene-Level Granularity

The fundamental question: what unit does the AI generate?

- **Sentence-level** (Sudowrite, TypeTak): Writer does all structural work. AI just finishes sentences.
- **Full auto-generation** (ChatGPT, standalone generators): No author control. Consistency dies after a few exchanges.
- **Scene-level** (Narrex): Writer builds the structure visually. AI generates prose within that structure, with full narrative context.

Scene-level is the sweet spot — enough context for coherent output, enough granularity for author control.

### NLE Timeline as Mental Model

The workspace is modeled after a video editor. The timeline has multi-track clips (scenes), a preview window (editor), and a media library (character map). This metaphor was chosen because:

- Writers naturally think in parallel storylines — multi-track supports this directly
- Scenes have extent (duration), not just position — enabling simultaneity detection across tracks
- The NLE metaphor is intuitive for digital-native users who've used Premiere, CapCut, or GarageBand

### Event-Episode Separation

Scenes (what happens in the story) and episodes (how it's packaged for readers) are independent layers. This is a critical product decision:

- Multiple small events can merge into one episode
- One large event can span multiple episodes
- Dragging an episode divider on the timeline re-partitions chapters, and the AI prompt auto-rebuilds

No other tool makes this distinction. Every competitor treats chapters as the primary unit.

### "Revise, Don't Write from Scratch"

The AI generates drafts. The author directs and refines. This framing is deliberate — it shifts the user's role from "writer who can't write" to "director who shapes the output." Direction-based editing ("add more tension", "increase dialogue") lowers the craft barrier without removing creative ownership.

## UX Design

### Workspace: Single Page, Panel-Based

No deep navigation. Users switch between panels, not pages. Every core action is at most 2 clicks from the workspace.

```
+---------------------------------------------------------------+
| Header · Project Title              [Config v]  [theme]  Saved |
+----------+------------------------------------+---------------+
| Character| Editor                             | Scene Detail  |
| Map      | (prose preview + editing)          | (metadata)    |
+----------+--------+---------------------------+---------------+
| Timeline (multi-track, horizontal scroll, drag-and-drop)      |
| Track 1: [scene]--[scene]--[scene]--[scene]--[+]             |
| Track 2:         [scene]--[scene]--[+]                        |
+---------------------------------------------------------------+
```

### Progressive Disclosure

Non-technical hobbyist writers are the primary users. The interface must not overwhelm.

| Step | Visible | Hidden | Revealed When |
|------|---------|--------|---------------|
| First load | Single-track timeline, config bar, character map | Multi-track, branch/merge, context menus | User interacts with timeline |
| First scene selected | Scene detail panel auto-opens | Direction-based editing, shortcuts | First draft generated |
| First draft generated | Standard text editing | "Edit with AI" | User selects text |

### Scene Visual States

Not color-only — each state uses fill pattern + icon + label for accessibility:

- **Empty** — hollow clip, no icon
- **AI Draft** — half-filled clip, pen icon
- **Edited** — solid clip, checkmark
- **Needs Revision** — filled with warning border, triangle icon (config changed since last draft)

### Auto-Save, No Save Button

All edits auto-save with 1-second debounce. Status indicator: "Saving..." → "All changes saved." Single-user product, so last-write-wins is sufficient.

## Competitive Positioning

8 tools reviewed (4 international, 4 Korean). None combine visual timeline + scene-level AI + Korean web novel specialization.

| Gap | International Tools | Korean Tools | Narrex |
|-----|-------------------|-------------|--------|
| Visual timeline | None offer it | None offer it | Multi-track NLE |
| AI generation unit | Sentence (Sudowrite) or none (Novelcrafter) | Full auto (standalone generators) or sentence (TypeTak) | Scene-level with full context |
| Korean web novel genres | English-only | Weak output quality | Built for regression, romance-fantasy, murim conventions |
| Author control | Varies | Minimal | Structure is the prompt — full control |

Cost per scene: ~$0.03–0.06 with prompt caching. Cost per complete 40-episode novel: ~$3.60–9.60. Free-to-paid conversion driven by users hitting the generation ceiling.

## Phase Strategy

**Phase 1 (current):** Core loop validation — idea → structure → generate → edit. Single question: do users keep editing the AI drafts, or discard them?

**Phase 2:** Episode organization layer, draft variations, tone sliders, export.

**Phase 3:** World map, temporal relationship tracking, revision tools, AI Surprise mode.
