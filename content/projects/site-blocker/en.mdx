**There is no unblock button.**

That sentence is the entire product. Every other site blocker ships with an escape hatch — a password, a timer, a "just 5 more minutes" dialog — and bets on you being rational in the exact moment you're least rational. Permanent Site Blocker removes the choice.

Block a site, and the only way back is to uninstall the extension entirely. Your blocklist vanishes. You start over from zero. That friction is the feature.

## Precise, not blunt

YouTube isn't the problem — Shorts is. Reddit isn't the problem — the front page is. Block exactly what distracts you:

- **Full domains** — `twitter.com` blocks everything, subdomains included
- **Specific paths** — `youtube.com/shorts` blocks Shorts but leaves the rest
- **Regex patterns** — `youtube\.com.*[가-힣]` blocks Korean YouTube content while keeping English videos

## Nothing leaves your browser

Zero analytics. Zero servers. Zero npm dependencies. The entire extension is ~300 lines of vanilla JavaScript — readable in 10 minutes, auditable by anyone. Your blocklist syncs across Chrome devices through Chrome's own storage API. Nothing else touches your data.

1,100+ daily active users. 5.0 average rating. The most common review: "I installed it because there's no unblock button."
