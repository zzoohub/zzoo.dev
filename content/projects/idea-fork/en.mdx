## Spent hours scrolling Reddit for product ideas?

You open 20 tabs. Skim r/SaaS, r/startups, r/selfhosted. Check Product Hunt for what just launched. Cross-reference app store reviews. Try to spot the gap between what people complain about and what exists.

Five hours later, you have a messy spreadsheet and a vague hunch. Maybe.

**The research is valuable. The manual process isn't.**

## Demand meets supply, automatically

Idea Fork runs a daily pipeline that does what you do manually — but across 44 subreddits, RSS feeds, App Store reviews, Play Store reviews, and Product Hunt — all at once.

It collects the **demand** (what people actually complain about), tracks the **supply** (what products exist), and uses AI to synthesize the **gap** into actionable product briefs. Every claim links back to the real source post. No hallucinations — AI with receipts.

## How it works

1. **Browse the Feed** — scroll through tagged user signals filtered by type (Need, Complaint, Feature Request) and category
2. **Explore Products** — see trending products paired with their user complaint signal counts
3. **Read AI Briefs** — get synthesized product opportunities with problem statements, demand signals, and solution directions
4. **Verify the sources** — every brief links back to the original posts, so you can judge for yourself

## The pipeline behind the scenes

Every day, a 5-stage pipeline runs automatically:

- **Fetch** — pulls from Reddit, RSS, App Store, Play Store, and Product Hunt in parallel
- **Tag** — Gemini Flash Lite classifies each post by type, sentiment, and topic tags
- **Score** — updates product trending scores and complaint counts
- **Cluster** — HDBSCAN groups related complaints, Gemini generates cluster labels
- **Synthesize** — Gemini Flash writes full briefs from demand-supply gaps, with source attribution

No manual curation. No editorial bias. Just data-driven opportunity discovery.
