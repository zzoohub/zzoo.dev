Every morning, a pipeline scans 44 subreddits, RSS feeds, App Store reviews, Play Store reviews, and Product Hunt. It tags every complaint by type and topic, tracks what products exist, clusters demand signals, and synthesizes the gaps into product briefs — each backed by links to the original posts.

The output: a feed of product opportunities where real user demand exists but current supply falls short. Not "AI says users are frustrated" — you click through and read what they actually wrote.

## The synthesis step no one automates

Finding complaints is easy. Connecting them to existing products and spotting what's missing — that's where ideas live. You could do it manually: 20 tabs, 5 hours, a messy spreadsheet, a vague hunch. GummySearch shows Reddit threads but needs manual queries and doesn't synthesize. SparkToro maps audiences, not product gaps. Exploding Topics shows growth, not what to build.

Idea Fork automates the full pipeline: collection, tagging, clustering, and synthesis. End-to-end, no manual step.

## Three ways in

- **Signal Feed** — raw complaints and feature requests, filtered by type and category. The demand side.
- **Product Tracker** — trending products with their complaint counts. The supply side, and where it's failing.
- **AI Briefs** — synthesized opportunities with problem statements, evidence, solution directions, and competitive landscape. Every claim links to its source.
