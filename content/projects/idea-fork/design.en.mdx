## The Insight

Every "find a startup idea" tool gives you ingredients and assumes you'll cook. GummySearch shows Reddit threads. SparkToro shows audiences. Exploding Topics shows trend graphs. They all stop at collection.

But complaints alone don't become ideas. The real bottleneck is the synthesis step — connecting what people complain about to what already exists, and spotting the gap between the two. That's where product opportunities live, and no tool goes there.

Idea Fork's bet: automate the full journey. Map demand against supply, then synthesize the gap into briefs — with every claim linking back to the original post.

## Who This Is For

**Jinho — the side-project scout**

Full-time developer, 10 hours a week to build something. He's shipped before and knows what "good enough to start" looks like. What he doesn't have is 5 hours to browse Reddit hunting for signal. He wants pre-digested opportunities that let him skip straight to "should I build this?"

**Sarah — the validation-first founder**

She's been burned by building before validating. "I think people want this" makes her flinch. She needs evidence: real complaints, real numbers, real source links. Before committing 3 months, she wants to see 47 people across 5 communities independently frustrated by the same problem.

## Product Decisions

### No login wall

No signup. No paywall. No onboarding flow. Open the site, see the feed.

This wasn't a UX preference — it was strategic. The product's value is only demonstrable by using it. A login wall before the core experience kills conversion. Let users browse 10 briefs first. Once hooked, premium features justify an account.

The trade-off: no personalization, no saved preferences, no notifications. The only write action in the MVP is rating briefs — via anonymous session cookies. No PII, no GDPR headache, no auth system to build.

### Three tabs, not just Briefs

Why not show only AI-generated briefs? Because trust requires transparency.

If all you see are AI summaries, you're trusting a black box. The Feed exists so users can see the raw material. Products exists so they can evaluate the supply landscape. Briefs are the synthesis; the other two tabs are the receipts.

The alternative: a single-page brief experience — simpler to build, easier to explain. But an AI product making unsourced claims about "market opportunities" felt indistinguishable from hallucination. Showing the full pipeline — raw data, supply context, synthesized output — earns trust that a polished summary alone cannot.

### Evidence-first AI

Every claim in a brief links to a real source post. This shaped the entire data architecture: briefs store `source_snapshots` for join-free rendering, with a normalized table underneath for referential integrity.

The tempting alternative: "Trust us, the AI analyzed 47 posts and concluded X." That works for ChatGPT where users expect unsourced opinions. It fails for a product whose value proposition is data-driven discovery. If a brief says "users across 5 communities are frustrated with mobile note-taking performance," you should be able to click through and read what they actually said.

### Mobile-first, content-first

No splash screen. No hero section. No welcome modal. The first thing you see is the latest complaint stream. Content is the interface.

Primary actions sit in the thumb zone. Mobile bottom tab bar mirrors the three core experiences. Cards show title, source badge, and sentiment indicator — long text is line-clamped at 2 lines with expand/collapse.

The rejected alternative: a marketing landing page before the product. But the pitch is more convincing when experienced than when described.

## Competitive Positioning

The key insight isn't better data collection — it's end-to-end coverage. Every competitor gives you one half: demand OR supply, raw data OR synthesis.

GummySearch goes deep on Reddit but needs manual queries and doesn't synthesize. SparkToro maps audiences but has no product opportunity path. Exploding Topics shows growth trends but not what to build about them.

Idea Fork covers the full pipeline. Raw signal to aggregation to synthesis to brief with source links. The synthesis step — where complaints become opportunities — is what no one else automates.
